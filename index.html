INTRODUCTION

This program is made for players new to chess to help facilitate their efforts in learning the game as quickly as possible. Big part of that is getting familiar with the name system of a chessboard, which is essential for reading books about chess and for spatial awareness.

Standard Chessboard

A chessboard is a coordinate system. Rows are identified by numbers, while columns are identified by the first eight letters of the alphabet. There are eight rows and eight columns, which makes for sixty-four sqaures. Each square is identified by its column name followed by its row number, like "a1" or "g7". The aim of this program is to embed position names in your mind, so they can become a part of your intuition and thus help you on your way to mastery of chess.

Names are absolute, meaning that the white and black players are naming the same sqaures with the same names. The closest row to the white player is number one while the closest one to the black player is number eight.
